Lymphatic Therapy

The importance of a healthy lymph system cannot be overstated. As one of the least understood systems of the body, its proper functioning is fundamental to optimal health.

Effective lymph therapy is very gentle touch that follows a specific protocol of direction and timing.

The Lymphatic System Components

Organs

Thymus, liver, tonsils, adenoids, heart and spleen.

Nodes

400-700 nodes in the human body. These little dynamos are on the frontline of our immune system. They recruit white blood cells, destroy pathogens (such as bacteria) and filter waste.

Lymph Fluid

The fluid is a transparent colorless alkaline liquid which contains: proteins, lipids, carbohydrates, enzymes, urea, minerals, hormones, toxins, bacteria, body waste, oxygen and nutrients.

Lymph Capillaries

Virtually all cells and tissue in the body are permeated with lymph capillaries that carry the lymph fluid.

Effects of Lymphatic Blockage

Because the lymph system cleanses nearly every cell in the body, the negative effects of chronic lymph blockage include, but are not limited to:

  • Frequent cold and flu-like symptoms
  • Joint pain
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fatigue
  • Mood swings and depression
  • Acne and cellulite

Important Safety Information

Contraindications include: acute inflammation, malignant tumors, thrombosis, and major heart issues.
